About

I am a robotics engineer and enthusiast, who has more than 3 years of working experience in embedded software programming, designing mechanical systems, and industrial prototyping, with a profound interest in both computer vision and machine learning.


Graduate Researcher Assistant - Perception and Motion Planning @ UCSD Autonomous Vehicle Laboratory

An infamous maxim that Alan Kay shared which deeply influenced me as a robotics engineer is "People who are serious about software should make their own hardware." With my years of work experience, I learned to build more efficient and optimized machines by having a clear understanding on how both the robotics hardware and software parts work together in a feedback loop. It also opens up my eyes in terms of innovation in the robotics field and motivates me to challenge the frontier of technology.

  • City: San Diego, California
  • Status: Open to Work

Currently, I am pursuing my Master of Science degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a specialization in Intelligent Systems, Robotics, and Control at the University of California, San Diego. I am also working on the perception and motion planning for autonomous micro-mobility vehicles as a Graduate Research Assistant at the UCSD Autonomous Vehicle Laboratory under the supervision of David Paz and Professor Henrik I. Christensen. I graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering with Summa Cum Laude from the Ohio State University and earned an Honor Research Distinction in Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering.

Previously, I was an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Engineer, who built and developed algriculture drones, Oryctes for Malaysia’s largest plantation companies, which are Sime Darby Plantation and Genting Plantation, at Poladrone. I also worked as a Robotics Research Assistant under the supervision of Professor Ran Dai at the OSU Automation and Optimization Lab not only to build and design a team of miniature wheeled robots with a jumping capability but also to implement a noble path planning algorithm that solves multiple traveling salesman problem.
